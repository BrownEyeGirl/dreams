# TASK 9

## BIKE GARAGE  
https://emilebedard.github.io/cart263/Projects/project2/interactive_bike_garage_ii/

This project is simply stunning. From impressive models to practical application, I really enjoyed using this bike part registration system. The 3D models are artful and polished, with clean white meshes and beautiful lighting that encourages the user to imagine taking the bike outdoors. I don’t own a bike, so I can only assume its accuracy, but knowing Emile and his passion for biking, it seems well-informed.

One piece of feedback is that the main page could be more interactive. The toolbox animations are engaging, but I’d like to see a stronger integration between the simple HTML elements and the 3D interactive components.

## BE NOT AFRAID CHILD  
https://seangregoryv8.github.io/cart263/projects/2/

The concept is original in its post-ironic composition, and it’s difficult to tell how serious it intends to be. Creatively, it’s interesting: it presents a 3D angel as an oracle or preacher that the user is unable to directly interact with. I appreciate the animations, the flashing biblical verses, and the robotic voice narration.

The ability to interact with the camera perspective is also interesting, though it feels somewhat disconnected from the overall intention. Two pieces of feedback would be to experiment more with the lighting to create a more cohesive visual experience, and to adjust the shadow so its coverage area is larger.

## CONSUME OR DIE  
https://aydanmcgrail.github.io/cart263_FinalProject/

Simple, yet beautiful and functional. The visual design and gameplay concept are very well executed. I do wish it were a bit easier to learn or included more of a grace period, though that may be part of the intended challenge.

The blood splatters and vague, ominous messaging effectively establish atmosphere and immersion. The unsettling ads and low-resolution graphics also reinforce the game’s tone. My favorite feature is how the player loses the ability to exit the screen during gameplay, creating a sense of being trapped until death.

Some feedback would be to further develop the narrative and add more animations for the opponent. Overall, I really like this project and appreciate how effectively it leverages its simplicity.
